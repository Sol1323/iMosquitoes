Why mosquitoes?

There are over 3xxx mosquito species on the planet, yet only yy species transmit pathogens to humans. Help build knowledge of the distribution and abundance of mosquitoes to help identify mosquitoes in your community and take measures to protect yourself from mosquito-borne pathogens.

Of the XX species 6 have been identified to transmit both human and wildlife pathogens.

  • Southern House Mosquito (Culex quinquefasciatus)
  • Yellow-fever Mosquito (Aedes aegypti)
  • Asian Tiger Mosquito (Aedes albopictus)
  • Floodwater Mosquito (Aedes vexans)
  • Asian Rock Pool Mosquito (Aedes japonicus)
  • Bromeliad Mosquito (Wyeomyia mitchelli)
